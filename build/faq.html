Themelio 101

What is Themelio?

Themelio is the first trustless blockchain protocol. Its foundational technology is immutable, eradicating the need for any governance model that brings politics, instability, and avoidable complexity. Think of Themelio as the new Internet Protocol, reinvented for Web3. As a low-level application-neutral network protocol, Themelio provides developers a root of trust where they can build a nearly limitless range of intermediary protocols capable of powering a new world of applications.

What does 'Root of Trust' mean? What does 'endogenous trust' mean?

A root of trust is the foundation on which all secure operations of a system depend. Endogenous trust means that the trust originates from and perpetuates from within the system itself, not from trust in the operators of the system. Themelio is a root of trust for a new paradigm of blockchain-based systems because it can promise endogenous trust to its users. This is achieved through radical simplicity and immutability. There are no protocol politics and no need to ever trust another party. Themelio is a simple, secure base layer that doesn't change.

Is Themelio a layer 1 blockchain?

Yes. Themelio is a layer 1 blockchain that does one thing provides robust low-level functionality critical to secure decentralized apps and does so very well. Its design is firmly centered around decoupling the blockchain from user-facing applications, unlike other layer 1 protocols which suffer from 'protocol-application.' The core blockchain logic is much simpler than other general-purpose blockchains like Ethereum and Tezos, employing immutability and therefore eliminating the need for governance.

Is Themelio Proof of Stake?

Yes, Themelio operates off a Proof-of-Stake consensus mechanism known as Synkletos. Synkletos not only serves to incentivize good behavior among stakeholders (also known as 'validators' in other PoS chains), but also serves a critical role in moderating the supply of SYM, one of Themelio's native tokens.

MEL and SYM Tokens

What are MEL and SYM?

MEL and SYM are the symbiotic tokens that fuel Themelio. MEL and SYM are interdependent, and both critical to unlocking Themelioe2's unique, trustless model. MEL is a fully decentralized low-volatility currency with a dynamic supply that can be liquidated for SYM tokens, the staking utility that undermines all activity on the Themelio network.

MEL serves as the fundamental currency of the Themelio ecosystem, and is an algorithmic stablecoin pegged to the network's computation, not to any fiat currency. SYM, which is used for staking and serves to ensure MEL's stability, is free to fluctuate as the market demands.

Learn more about MEL, SYM, and Themelio's Tokenomics.

What is an algorithmic stablecoin?

An algorithmic stablecoin is a cryptocurrency that remains stable by using a trustless issuance model rather than pegging the stablecoin to a fiat currency. MEL is the algorithmic stablecoin native to Themelio. It is distributed by Melmint, the first trustless value-stable cryptocurrency issuance model.

Melmint's algorithm pegs MEL to "day of sequential computing" (DOSC), a unit measuring the cost of sequential computation for a day. This is done by combining Elasticoin, an existing algorithm for measuring the value of a DOSC, with a strong implicit reserve based on diluting shares of fee revenue. Unlike all other stablecoin proposals, Melmint operates without any trusted issuers or oracles while maintaining a robust peg, solving a major open problem in cryptocurrency design.

The main objective of Melmint is to hold the price of 1 MEL = 1 DOSC. There are two simultaneous processes: an auction that establishes the value of a SYM in DOSC, and a MEL/SYM exchange guarantee that backs every 1 MEL with 1 DOSC worth of SYM.

This complex arrangement ensures that MEL is a value-stable cryptocurrency while SYM, the staking utility, may fluctuate in value over time.

Are there validators on Themelio?

Yes. Those who stake on Themelio are known as stakeholders'. Stakeholders stake SYM to participate in Themelio's Proof of Stake consensus mechanism, Synkletos. Ahead of mainnet launch, join the Themelio network as a validator and contribute to the network either as a nonvoting auditor node or a voting staker node.
